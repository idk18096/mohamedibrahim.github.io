About Our Project

The Civil Action Project is focused on raising awareness and mobilizing our community to take action on homelessness. We highlight the challenges, leverage research, and drive volunteer-led initiatives to support our neighbors in need.

Our Mission

We aim to: educate on root causes of homelessness, partner with local support services, and advocate for systemic solutions such as affordable housing and comprehensive support systems for individuals and families.

Key Homelessness Facts (USA)

  • On a single night in January 2024, approximately 770,000 people experienced homelessness in the U.S. — an 18 % increase from 2023. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Approximately one in three individuals experiencing homelessness (≈ 152,585 people) were considered chronically homeless. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Families with children facing homelessness saw a dramatic 39 % increase between 2023 and 2024. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • The homeless population is disproportionately people of color: Black people represent ~12 % of the U.S. population but ~32 % of all people experiencing homelessness in 2024. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
