HUD-backed Solutions & Programs

Below are widely used HUD programs and approaches that communities use to reduce homelessness. Hover or click each card to read more.

Continuum of Care (CoC)

Local CoCs coordinate housing and services for people experiencing homelessness.

CoC supports local planning and grants for transitional housing, permanent supportive housing, & rapid re-housing. Learn more at HUD sources included below.

Emergency Housing Vouchers (EHV)

Short-term vouchers to move people quickly into permanent housing.

EHV connects households to rental assistance and housing search support to reduce time spent unsheltered or in temporary shelter.

Homeless Assistance Grants

Federal grants fund shelter operations, prevention, and supportive services.

Grants help shelter providers stabilize operations and increase capacity for outreach and housing placement programs.

Prevention & Rapid Re-Housing

Programs that stop evictions and speed households back into stable housing.

Support includes rental assistance, case management, and short-term financial help to prevent homelessness.

Reflection & Project Overview

Below are two subsections that outline the issue of homelessness, its causes and effects, and reflections on civic action and solutions.

Proof of Action

As part of my civic action, I reached out to leaders and organizations: including senators, local representatives, and community services. This was to raise awareness about homelessness and housing challenges in Colorado. Below is my documented proof of action.

Project Overview

1. Identify and describe the problem you have identified in your community, Colorado, or the U.S.

1b: The problem I focused on is homelessness, which continues to rise across the United States and Colorado. Many people struggle to find affordable housing or safe shelter, leaving thousands without a stable home.

2. Why did you choose this topic?

2b: I chose this topic because homelessness is becoming a major issue that affects real people in our communities. I want others to understand that homeless individuals are people like us who deserve compassion and support.

3. What are the main causes of this problem?

3b: Some main causes include the rising cost of housing, job insecurity, mental health challenges, and the lack of affordable healthcare and support systems for vulnerable populations.

4. What are the most significant effects of this problem?

4b: The effects include increased poverty, health issues, and community safety concerns. Homelessness also creates long-term challenges for families and individuals trying to rebuild their lives.

5. Who is affected/involved? How?

5b: This problem affects low-income families, veterans, individuals with disabilities, and those facing mental health struggles. It also affects the larger community, which must address public health, housing, and social service needs.

6. What core value explains why the government should act? Explain.

6b: The core value is equality and justice. Everyone deserves a safe place to live and the opportunity to rebuild their life. The government has a responsibility to protect citizens‚Äô welfare and promote access to housing for all.

7. Why is your problem something the government can/should address?

7b: Homelessness is a national and local crisis that requires policy changes, funding, and coordination between federal, state, and local governments. The government can create affordable housing programs, expand healthcare access, and support organizations working to end homelessness.

Action Reflection

1. How did your civic action help improve, fix, or address the issue/problem your project focused on?

1b: My civic action helped raise awareness about homelessness by sharing data, solutions, and personal stories through my website. It encourages others to view homelessness with empathy and understand the importance of supporting housing programs.

2. What else could you do in the future to help fix this issue/problem?

2b: In the future, I could expand this website to include local resources, volunteer opportunities, and donation links to shelters and HUD-supported programs to create more direct impact.

3. What outside obstacles did you face in completing this project?

3b: Some obstacles I faced included finding reliable data sources, designing an effective website layout, and making sure the information I shared was accurate and supported by official organizations like HUD.

4. What surprised you about the process of being civically active?

4b: I was surprised by how much effort it takes to create awareness and how few people actually understand the challenges homeless individuals face. It made me realize the importance of civic involvement and speaking up for others.

5. What recommendations would you have for someone who wanted to continue the action you started?

5b: I would recommend staying informed, continuing to share information online, and reaching out to local officials or organizations. Even small actions like spreading awareness can make a big difference over time.
